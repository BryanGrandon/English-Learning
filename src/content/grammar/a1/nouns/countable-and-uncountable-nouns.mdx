# Countable Nouns

Countable nouns are nouns that **can be counted**. They have **singular** and **plural** forms.

<Grammar
  type="rule"
  title="Countable Nouns"
  description="Use countable nouns for people, animals, objects, and things that you can count."
  highlight='Singular ↔ Plural'
>

- #### Examples
  - one **book** → two **books**
  - one **apple** → three **apples**
  - one **student** → five **students**
  - one **chair** → four **chairs**

</Grammar>

<Callout type="tip">
Countable nouns can be used with **a**, **an**, **numbers**, and **many**.

- a book
- an apple
- three students
- many chairs

</Callout>

---

## Uncountable Nouns

Uncountable nouns are nouns that _cannot be counted individually_. They usually represent **substances, materials, liquids, or abstract ideas** and _do not have plural forms_.

<Grammar
  type="rule"
  title="Uncountable Nouns"
  description="Use uncountable nouns for things that are measured, not counted."
  highlight='Uncountable Noun'
>

- #### Examples
  - water
  - milk
  - rice
  - sugar
  - information
  - money

</Grammar>

<Callout type="tip">
Uncountable nouns are **not** used with **a** or **an**.

- ✅ some water
- ✅ much money
- ❌ a water
- ❌ an information

</Callout>

---

## Grammar Rules

### Plural Forms

Countable nouns have singular and plural forms. (**_Singular → Plural_**)

<ContentGrid>
<Callout type='example'>

- **egg** → **_eggs_**
- **bicycle** → **_bicycles_**
- **dress** → **_dresses_**

</Callout>
</ContentGrid>
---

### Use a / an

Use **a** or **an** with singular countable nouns. (**_a / an + Singular Countable Noun_**)

<ContentGrid>
<Callout type='example'>

- an **apple**
- a **house**

</Callout>

<Callout type="important">

- ❌ a **milk**
- ✅ **milk**

</Callout>
</ContentGrid>

---

### Use Numbers

Use numbers with countable nouns. (**_Number + Countable Noun_**)

<ContentGrid>
<Callout type='example'>

- three **apples**
- five **houses**

</Callout>

<Callout type="important">

- ❌ two **waters**
- ✅ two **bottles of water**

</Callout>
</ContentGrid>

---

<Callout type='important'>
  Countable nouns can be used with **a**, **an**, **numbers**, **many**, and **a few**.
</Callout>
---

## Making Uncountable Nouns Countable

Sometimes we can _make uncountable nouns countable_ using **measure expressions**.

<ContentGrid>

| Expression | Example          |
| ---------- | ---------------- |
| a piece of | a piece of bread |
| a cup of   | a cup of tea     |
| a glass of | a glass of wine  |
| a gram of  | a gram of salt   |

<Callout type="example_sentences">
  
- I want **a cup of tea**.
- She bought **a piece of bread**.

</Callout>
</ContentGrid>
