# Cardinal Numbers

Cardinal numbers tell us **how many** people or things there are. They are used to count and express quantity.

### Common Uses

- Age.
- Dates.
- Prices.
- Quantities.
- Phone numbers.

---

## Teens Cardinal Numbers

The numbers 13 through 19 follow a simple pattern. They all end in **-teen**, which represents the numbers 13 through 19.

<Grammar
  type="rule"
  title="Forming Teen Numbers"
  description="Most teen numbers are formed by adding _-teen_ to the base number."
  highlight='Base number + **_-teen_** = Teen number'
>

- #### _Examples_
  - four + **_teen_** → _fourteen_
  - six + **_teen_** → _sixteen_
  - seven + **_teen_** → _seventeen_

</Grammar>

## Tens Cardinal Number

The numbers from 20 to 90 are the multiples of ten in English. Learning them will help you form larger numbers, such as twenty-one, thirty-five, and ninety-nine.

<Grammar
  type="rule"
  title="Forming Tens"
  description="The multiples of ten usually end in _-ty_."
  highlight='Base number + **_-ty_** = Tens number'
>

- #### _Examples_
  - six + **_ty_** → _sixty_
  - seven + **_ty_** → _seventy_

</Grammar>

## Compound Cardinal Numbers

The numbers from 21 to 99 are formed by combining a multiple of ten with a single-digit number.

<Grammar
  type="rule"
  title="Forming Compound Numbers"
  description="Compound numbers from 21 to 99 are formed by combining the tens and the ones with a hyphen (-)."
  highlight='Tens + **_-_** + One = Compound number'
>

- #### _Examples_
  - twenty + **_-_** + one → _twenty-one_
  - thirty + **_-_** + five → _thirty-five_
  - sixty + **_-_** + eight → _sixty-eight_

</Grammar>

## Hundreds, Thousands, Millions

Learn how to express larger numbers in English by combining hundreds, thousands, and millions.

<Grammar
  type="rule"
  title="Combining Large Numbers"
  description="Large numbers are formed by combining a cardinal number with hundred, thousand, or million."
  highlight='Cardinal number + hundred / thousand / million'
>

- #### _Examples_
  - one + **_hundred_** → _one hundred_
  - two + **_thousand_** → _two thousand_
  - ten + **_million_** → _ten million_

</Grammar>

---

<Callout type='remember'>

**Hundred**, **thousand**, and **million** do not take **_-s_** when they follow a number.

</Callout>

### Using "and" in Numbers

<Grammar
  type="rule"
  title="Using and in Large Numbers"  
   description="British and American English use 'and' differently when reading large numbers."
>

- #### British English
  - In British English, **and** is used _before tens or units_.
    - one hundred **and** twenty (120)
    - two thousand **and** five (2,005)

- #### America English
  - American English often omits it.
    - one hundred twenty (120)
    - two thousand five (2,005)

</Grammar>
