# Adjectives

An **adjective** is a word that _describes or modifies a noun_. It gives more information about a **person, place, thing, or idea**.

Adjectives answer questions such as:

- _What kind?_
  - a **beautiful** flower
- _Which one?_
  - this **blue** car
- _How many?_
  - three **small** boxes

<ContentGrid>
<Callout type='example'>

- a **big** house
- two **tall** men
- **long** hair

</Callout>
<Callout type='example_sentences'>

- The **tall** man is my teacher.
- She has a **happy** child.
- The **red** ball is on the table.

</Callout>
</ContentGrid>

## Types of Adjectives

English adjectives can be divided into **six main types**. Each type has a different purpose and helps describe nouns in a specific way.

  <SectionCard

  >
    - Descriptive (Qualitative)
    - Quantitative
    - Demonstrative
    - Possessive
    - Interrogative
    - Distributive
  </SectionCard>

  <Callout type="important">
    Learning these types will help you describe people, places, things, and ideas more accurately.

## Position of Adjectives

In English, adjectives _usually come before the noun_ they describe.

<Grammar
  type="rule"
  title="Position of Adjectives"
  description="In English, adjectives usually come before the noun they describe."
  highlight='Adjective + Noun'
>

- #### Examples
  - a **_red_** car
  - a **_small_** house
  - an **_interesting_** book
  - two **_happy_** children

</Grammar>

---

<Grammar
  type="rule"
  title="After Linking Verbs"
  description="Some adjectives come after linking verbs instead of before a noun."
>

- #### Common Linking Verbs
  - to be
  - to feel
  - to look
  - to seem
  - to smell
  - to taste

</Grammar>

## Adjective Rules

These are some important rules about adjectives in English.

### No Gender Changes

Adjectives do not change for masculine or feminine nouns. **Same adjective**

<ContentGrid>
<Callout type="example_sentences" >

- a **tall** man
- a **tall** woman

</Callout>
</ContentGrid>

---

### No Plural Forms

Adjectives usually have the same form in singular and plural. **Same adjective**

<ContentGrid>
<Callout type="example_sentences" >

- a **red** car
- two **red** cars

</Callout>
</ContentGrid>

---

### Multiple Adjectives

More than one adjective can be used to describe the same noun.

<ContentGrid>
<Grammar
  type="rule"
  title="General Rule"
  highlight='Adjective + Adjective + Noun'
>

- #### Examples
  - a **small black** book
  - a **pretty Spanish** woman
  - a **large glass** table

</Grammar>
</ContentGrid>

## Order of Adjectives

When using multiple adjectives, English usually follows this order.

| Order | Type        | Examples             |
| ----- | ----------- | -------------------- |
| 1     | opinion     | beautiful, wonderful |
| 2     | size        | big, small, tall     |
| 3     | age         | old, young, new      |
| 4     | shape       | round, square        |
| 5     | color       | red, blue, green     |
| 6     | origin      | Spanish, American    |
| 7     | material    | cotton, silver       |
| 8     | purpose/use | electric, political  |
