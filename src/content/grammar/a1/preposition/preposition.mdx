# Prepositions

Prepositions are words that show the relationship between other words in a sentence.

They can express **time, place, position, movement, or direction**.

They are very common but can be difficult because _their meaning depends on context and they do not always translate directly_.

---

## Position Rule

Prepositions are usually placed **before a noun, pronoun, or -ing form**.

<Grammar
  type="structure"
  title="Basic Structure"
  description="A preposition introduces the word or phrase that follows it."
  highlight="Verb + preposition + noun / pronoun / -ing"
>

- #### Examples
  - The cat is **in** the box.
  - The book is **on** the table.
  - She is **at** the airport.
  - Think **about** it.
  - Wash your hands **before** eating.

</Grammar>

<Callout type="important">

After a preposition, use:

- ✔ **a noun** → _in the room_
- ✔ **a pronoun** → _with him_
- ✔ **an -ing form** → _before eating_
- ❌ **a base verb** → _before eat_

</Callout>

## Common Prepositions

<ContentGrid>

<SectionCard title="In">

Used for something **inside** or **within** a place or period of time.

- #### Use for
  - months, years, and longer periods
  - enclosed spaces
  - cities and countries

- #### Examples
  - in a room
  - in Chile
  - in May
  - in the morning

</SectionCard>

<SectionCard title="At">

Used for a **specific point** in place or time.

- #### Use for
  - exact places
  - specific times

- #### Examples
  - at home
  - at the airport
  - at 8:00
  - at Christmas

</SectionCard>

<SectionCard title="On">

Used for **surfaces** and **specific days or dates**.

- #### Use for
  - surfaces
  - days and dates

- #### Examples
  - on the wall
  - on Monday
  - on July 5th
  - on my birthday

</SectionCard>

</ContentGrid>

## Types of Prepositions

### Prepositions of Place

Show **where** someone or something is.

<Callout type="example">

- The book is **on** the table.
- The cat is **in** the box.

</Callout>

<Callout type="note">

They are often used after the verb **be**.

</Callout>

---

### Prepositions of Time

Show **when** something happens.

<Callout type="example">

- in June
- on Monday
- at 8 o'clock

</Callout>

<Callout type='note'>

The most common are **in**, **on**, and **at**.

</Callout>

---

### Prepositions of Movement

Show **direction** or **movement**.

<Callout type="example">

- go **to**
- walk **through**
- jump **over**

</Callout>
<Callout type="note">

They describe movement from one place to another.

</Callout>

> **Note:** They describe movement from one place to another.

## Common Mistakes

<ContentGrid>
<Grammar type="common_mistake" title='Translating prepositions literally from Spanish'>

- ❌ I live **in** the street
- ✅ I live **on** this street

</Grammar>

<Grammar type="common_mistake" title='Using a verb after a preposition'>

- ❌ before eat
- ✅ before **eating**

</Grammar>
</ContentGrid>
