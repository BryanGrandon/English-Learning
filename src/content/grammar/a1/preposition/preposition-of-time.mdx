# Prepositions of Time

Prepositions of time indicate **when** something happens.

<Callout type="example">

- I usually have breakfast **before** work.
- We went home **after** the movie.

</Callout>

---

## Position in the Sentence

Prepositions of time usually follow this pattern:

<Grammar
  type="structure"
  title="Basic Structure"
  description="Place the preposition after the verb and before a noun or time expression."
  highlight="Verb + preposition + time expression"
>

- #### Examples
  - Call me **before** one.
  - We will meet **after** the movie.
  - She always studies **before** dinner.
  - They left **after** the meeting.

</Grammar>

Prepositions of time normally come **_after the verb_** and _before a noun or time expression_.

---

## Common Prepositions of Time

<Vocabulary title="Common Prepositions of Time" description="Learn the most common prepositions used to talk about when something happens." level='A1' words={4}>

| Preposition | Meaning                       | Example                                 |
| ----------- | ----------------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| before      | Earlier than a time or event. | Call me **before** one.                 |
| after       | Later than a time or event.   | We will meet **after** the movie.       |
| during      | Throughout a period or event. | Don't talk **during** the meeting.      |
| for         | For a length of time.         | I lived in England **for** three years. |

</Vocabulary>

---

## Common Confusion

<Grammar type="comparison" title='During vs For' description='Although both refer to time, they are used differently.'>

<SectionCard title='During'>

Use **during** to describe **when** something happens within a period or event.

- #### Examples
  - Don't talk **during** the movie.
  - She fell asleep **during** the lesson.

</SectionCard>

<SectionCard title='For'>

Use **for** to describe **how long** something lasts.

- #### Examples
  - I studied **for** two hours.
  - They lived there **for** five years.

</SectionCard>

</Grammar>
