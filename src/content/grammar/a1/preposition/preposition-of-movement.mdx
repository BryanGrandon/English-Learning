# Prepositions of Movement

**Prepositions of movement** (also called **_prepositions of direction_**) show _movement from one place to another_.

They are commonly used with **verbs of movement**, such as:

- go
- walk
- run
- fly
- drive
- swim
- climb

<Callout type="remember">

Prepositions of movement usually come **after the verb** and _before the destination or place_.

</Callout>

---

## Position in the Sentence

Most sentences with **prepositions of movement** follow this pattern.

<Grammar
  type="structure"
  title="Sentence Pattern"
  highlight="Subject + verb of movement + preposition + place"
>

- #### Examples
  - She walked **through the park**.
  - They ran **across the street**.
  - We drove **into the city**.
  - He jumped **over the fence**.

</Grammar>

## Common Prepositions of Movement

<Vocabulary title='Common Prepositions of Movement' description='Learn the most common prepositions used to express movement or direction.' level='A1' words={16} >

| Word        | Meaning                                      | Example                                     |
| ----------- | -------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| **to**      | Toward a destination.                        | We walked **to** the train station.         |
| **into**    | From outside to inside.                      | The cat jumped **into** the box.            |
| **out of**  | From inside to outside.                      | She came **out of** the library.            |
| **onto**    | To a surface.                                | The boy climbed **onto** the roof.          |
| **off**     | Away from a surface.                         | The picture fell **off** the wall.          |
| **through** | From one side to the other inside something. | We drove **through** the tunnel.            |
| **across**  | From one side to the other.                  | They walked **across** the bridge.          |
| **over**    | Above and across.                            | The plane flew **over** the mountains.      |
| **along**   | Following the length of something.           | We strolled **along** the beach.            |
| **under**   | Below something.                             | The rabbit ran **under** the fence.         |
| **up**      | Toward a higher place.                       | She climbed **up** the ladder.              |
| **down**    | Toward a lower place.                        | He rolled **down** the hill.                |
| **past**    | Beyond someone or something.                 | She walked **past** the post office.        |
| **around**  | In a circular direction.                     | They ran **around** the playground.         |
| **towards** | In the direction of.                         | The children ran **towards** their teacher. |

</Vocabulary>

## Common Mistakes

<ContentGrid>

<Grammar type='common_mistake' title='Confusing into and in'>

- **into** → movement inside
- **in** → position (no movement)
- #### Examples.
  - She walked **into** the room.
  - She is **in** the room.

</Grammar>

<Grammar type='common_mistake' title='Confusing onto and on'>

- **onto** → movement to a surface
- **on** → position
- #### Examples.
  - The cat jumped **onto** the table.
  - The cat is **on** the table.

</Grammar>

</ContentGrid>
