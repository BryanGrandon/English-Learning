# Prepositions of Place

Prepositions of place describe the location or position of a person, object, or place.

They usually come after the main verb, especially the verb **to be**.

<Grammar
  type="structure"
  title="Sentence Pattern"
  description="Use this pattern to describe the location of people or things."
  highlight="Subject + be + preposition + place"
>

- #### Example
  - The book is **on** the table.
  - The keys are **under** the chair.
  - She is **next to** the window.

</Grammar>

---

## Common Prepositions

### Nearby & Relative Position

| Preposition      | Meaning                      | Example                                           |
| ---------------- | ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| next to / beside | At the side of.              | The supermarket is **next to** the bank.          |
| by               | Close to.                    | I always sit **by** the window.                   |
| between          | In the middle of two things. | The shop is **between** the bank and the station. |

---

### Front & Back Position

| Preposition | Meaning           | Example                                   |
| ----------- | ----------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| behind      | At the back of.   | The church is **behind** the school.      |
| in front of | At the front of.  | The hotel is **in front of** the station. |
| opposite    | Facing something. | The bank is **opposite** the market.      |

---

### Vertical Position

| Preposition | Meaning                         | Example                                     |
| ----------- | ------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| under       | Lower than; directly below.     | The ball is **under** the chair.            |
| above       | Higher than; not touching.      | The clock is **above** the table.           |
| below       | Lower than; not directly under. | The basement is **below** the ground floor. |

---

## Key Differences

<ContentGrid>

<SectionCard title="Next to / Beside vs By">

- **Next to / Beside** → directly at the side of something.
- **By** → close to something, but not necessarily at its side.

</SectionCard>

<SectionCard title="In Front of vs Opposite">

- **In front of** → ahead of something, but not necessarily facing it.
- **Opposite** → directly facing something across from it.

</SectionCard>

<SectionCard title="Under vs Below">

- **Under** → directly beneath something.
- **Below** → at a lower level, not necessarily directly beneath.

</SectionCard>

<SectionCard title="Above vs On">

- **On** → touching a surface.
- **Above** → higher than something, without touching it.

</SectionCard>

</ContentGrid>
