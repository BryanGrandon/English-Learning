# Prepositions of Movement

**Prepositions of movement** (also called **_prepositions of direction_**) show _movement from one place to another_.

They are commonly used with **verbs of movement**, such as:

- go
- walk
- run
- fly
- drive
- swim
- climb

<Callout type="remember">

Prepositions of movement usually come **after the verb** and _before the destination or place_.

</Callout>

---

## Position in the Sentence

Most sentences with **prepositions of movement** follow this pattern.

<Grammar
  type="structure"
  title="Sentence Pattern"
  highlight="Subject + verb of movement + preposition + place"
>

- #### Examples
  - She walked **through the park**.
  - They ran **across the street**.
  - We drove **into the city**.
  - He jumped **over the fence**.

</Grammar>

## Common Prepositions of Movement

| Word    | Meaning                                      | Example                           |
| ------- | -------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| to      | Toward a destination.                        | She is going to the store.        |
| into    | From outside to inside.                      | He walked into the room.          |
| out off | From inside to outside.                      | 'He ran out of the house.         |
| onto    | To a surface.                                | The child climbed onto the chair. |
| off     | Away from a surface.                         | The book fell off the table.      |
| through | From one side to the other inside something. | She walked through the tunnel.    |
| across  | From one side to the other.                  | They swam across the river.       |
| over    | Above and across.                            | The bird flew over the trees.     |
| along   | a lo largo de                                | We walked along the river.        |
| under   | Below something                              | The dog crawled under the fence.  |
| up      | Toward a higher place.                       | She ran up the stairs.            |
| down    | Toward a lower place.                        | They walked down the hill.        |
| along   | Following the length of something.           | We cycled along the beach.        |
| past    | Beyond someone or something.                 | He drove past the supermarket.    |
| around  | In a circular direction.                     | They walked around the lake.      |
| towards | In the direction of.                         | She walked towards the station.   |

## **_Common Mistakes_**

**_Confusing into and in_**

- **into** → movement inside
- **in** → position (no movement)

📌 _Examples._

- She walked **into** the room.
- She is **in** the room.

---

**_Confusing onto and on_**

- **onto** → movement to a surface
- **on** → position

📌 _Examples._

- The cat jumped **onto** the table.
- The cat is **on** the table.
