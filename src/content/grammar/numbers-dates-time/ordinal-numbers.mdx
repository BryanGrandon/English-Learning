# What are Ordinal Numbers?

Ordinal numbers are used to show the _position or order_ of something in a sequence.

## How to Form Ordinal Numbers

Most ordinal numbers are formed by adding -th to the cardinal number. However, there are a few important exceptions and spelling changes to remember.

<Grammar type="rule" title="General Rule" description="Most ordinal numbers are formed by adding -th to the cardinal number.">

Cardinal number + **_-th_**

</Grammar>

<Callout type='tip'>Remember to add -th to most cardinal numbers to form ordinal numbers.</Callout>

### Exceptions (memorize)

The first three ordinal numbers are irregular and must be memorized.

    - one → **first**
    - two → **second**
    - three → **third**

### Spelling Changes

Some numbers change their spelling before adding **-th**.

| Cardinal | Ordinal |
| -------- | ------- |
| five     | fifth   |
| nine     | ninth   |
| twelve   | twelfth |

## Compound Ordinals (21–99)

Compound ordinal numbers are formed by combining a multiple of ten with an ordinal number. Only the last word changes to its ordinal form.

<LessonBlock title='General Rule' icon='rule' container='aside' heading='p'>
  - tens + hyphen (-) + ordinal number
</LessonBlock>
<LessonBlock title='Example' icon='example' container='section' heading='p'>

- 21 → twenty-first
- 32 → thirty-second
- 45 → forty-fifth
- 58 → fifty-eighth
- 99 → ninety-ninth

</LessonBlock>

### Remember

Only the last number becomes an ordinal.

- twenty-one → twenty-first
- thirty-two → thirty-second
- forty-five → forty-fifth

---

## Ordinal Abbreviations

Ordinal numbers can be written in a short form by adding the last letters of the ordinal word to the number.

<LessonBlock title='General Rule' icon='rule' container='aside' heading='p'>
  - number + ordinal ending
</LessonBlock>
<LessonBlock title='Example' icon='example' container='section' heading='p'>

- 1st → first
- 2nd → second
- 3rd → third
- 4th → fourth
- 21st → twenty-first

</LessonBlock>

### Remember

The ending depends on the last digit of the number.

<LessonColumns className='sm:grid-cols-2 lg:grid-cols-3'>
  <LessonBlock title='Spelling Rules' icon='rule' container='aside' heading='p'>
    - 1 → st (except 11th)
    - 2 → nd (except 12th)
    - 3 → rd (except 13th)
    - 4–9, 0 → th
  </LessonBlock>
  <LessonBlock title='Example' icon='example' container='section' heading='p'>

## Uses of Ordinal Numbers

Ordinal numbers are used to show the order, position, or date of people and things.

---

### Dates

Ordinal numbers are commonly used when saying or writing dates.

- #### Examples
  - April 5th
  - the first of May
  - July 21st
  - October 31st

---

### Position and Ranking

Use ordinal numbers to show someone's position in a race, competition, or list.

- #### Examples
  - He is second in the competition.
  - She finished first.
  - Our team came third.

---

### Floors and Order

Ordinal numbers are also used to describe floors, levels, chapters, pages, and the order of items.

- #### Examples
  - She lives on the third floor.
  - Turn to the fifth page.
  - Read the first chapter.
  - This is my second attempt.
