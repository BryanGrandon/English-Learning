# What are Ordinal Numbers?

Ordinal numbers are used to show the position or order of people or things in a sequence.

<LessonGrid className='sm:grid-cols-2'>

| Cardinal | Ordinal |
| -------- | ------- |
| one      | first   |
| two      | second  |
| three    | third   |
| four     | fourth  |
| five     | fifth   |

<Callout type="example_sentences">

1. She won **_first_** place in the competition.
2. My birthday is on the **_second_** of May.
3. He is the **_third_** student in the line.
4. We live on the **_fourth_** floor.
5. Today is the **_fifth_** day of the course.

</Callout>
</LessonGrid>

## How to Form Ordinal Numbers

Most ordinal numbers are formed by adding -th to the cardinal number. However, some ordinal numbers are irregular or have spelling changes.

<Grammar type="rule" title="General Rule" description="Most ordinal numbers are formed by adding **_-th_** to the cardinal number.">
Cardinal number + **_-th_** = Ordinal number

- #### _Example_
  - four + **_th_** → _fourth_
  - six + **_th_** → _sixth_
  - ten + **_th_** → _tenth_

</Grammar>

---

### Exceptions

The first three ordinal numbers are irregular.

| Cardinal | Ordinal |
| -------- | ------- |
| one      | first   |
| two      | second  |
| three    | third   |

---

### Spelling Changes

Some numbers change their spelling before adding **_-th_**.

| Cardinal | Ordinal |
| -------- | ------- |
| five     | fifth   |
| nine     | ninth   |
| twelve   | twelfth |

## Compound Ordinals

Compound ordinal numbers are formed by combining a multiple of ten with an ordinal number. Only the last word changes to its ordinal form.

<Grammar type="rule" title="General Rule" description="Most ordinal numbers are formed by adding -th to the cardinal number.">

tens + hyphen (-) + ordinal number

</Grammar>

<Vocabulary data={ORDINAL_NUMBERS.compound} />

### Remember

Only the last number becomes an ordinal.

- twenty-one → twenty-first
- thirty-two → thirty-second
- forty-five → forty-fifth

---

## Ordinal Abbreviations

Ordinal numbers can be written in a short form by adding the last letters of the ordinal word to the number.

<Grammar type="rule" title=" Ordinal Suffixes" description="When ordinal numbers are written as numerals, use these suffixes.">

- 1 → **_st_** (except **11th**)
- 2 → **_nd_** (except **12th**)
- 3 → **_rd_** (except **13th**)
- 4-20 → **_th_**
- Numbers ending in 4-9 or 0 → **_th_**

</Grammar>

| Ordinal      | Abbreviations |
| ------------ | ------------- |
| first        | 1st           |
| second       | 2nd           |
| third        | 3rd           |
| fourth       | 4th           |
| twenty-first | 21st          |

## Uses of Ordinal Numbers

Ordinal numbers are used to show the order, position, or date of people and things.

### Dates

Use ordinal numbers when saying or writing dates.

<LessonGrid className='sm:grid-cols-2'>
<Callout type="example">

- April 5th
- the first of May
- July 21st
- October 31st

</Callout>
</LessonGrid>

---

### Position and Ranking

Use ordinal numbers to show someone's position in a race, competition, or list.

<LessonGrid className='sm:grid-cols-2'>
<Callout type="example_sentences">

- She finished first in the race.
- He is second in the competition.
- Our team came third.
- I was the fourth person to arrive.

</Callout>
</LessonGrid>

---

### Floors and Order

Use ordinal numbers to describe the order of things, such as floors, chapters, pages, lessons, or attempts.

<LessonGrid className='sm:grid-cols-2'>
<Callout type="example_sentences">

- She lives on the third floor.
- Turn to the fifth page.
- Read the first chapter.
- This is my second attempt.

</Callout>
</LessonGrid>
