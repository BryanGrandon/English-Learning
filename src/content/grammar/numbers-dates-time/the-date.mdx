# Date

Dates are used to express the day, month, and year.
They help us talk about birthdays, holidays, appointments, events, and other important moments.
Learning how to read, write, and say dates correctly is an essential skill in everyday English.

<LessonGrid className='sm:grid-cols-2'>
  <Callout type="example_sentences">

    - My birthday is on May 12th.
    - The meeting is on October 5th.
    - School starts on March 1st.

  </Callout>
</LessonGrid>

## Writing Dates

Dates can be written in two common ways.

<LessonGrid className='sm:grid-cols-2'>
  <TopicBlock title='British English (UK)' icon={Flag} >
    **_Day + Month + Year_**

    - #### With _Words_
      - 15 October 2024

    - #### With _Numbers_
      - 15/10/2024

  </TopicBlock>
  <TopicBlock title='American English (US)' icon={Flag} >
    **_Month + Day + Year_**

    - #### With _Words_
      - October 15, 2024

    - #### With _Numbers_
      - 10/15/2024

  </TopicBlock>
</LessonGrid>

## How to Say the Date

When speaking dates in English, we usually use _ordinal numbers_.

<LessonGrid className='sm:grid-cols-2'>
  <Callout type="example_sentences">

    - It's the fifteenth of October (UK)
    - It's October the fifteenth (US)

  </Callout> 
</LessonGrid>

## Asking About the Date

- #### Common questions:
  - What's the date today?
  - What date is it?

- #### Possible answers:
  - It's the first of June.
  - It's June the first.

## Saying Years

Years are usually spoken differently from ordinary numbers.

| Year | Spoken                    |
| ---- | ------------------------- |
| 1999 | nineteen ninety-nine      |
| 2005 | two thousand and five     |
| 2018 | two thousand and eighteen |
| 2024 | twenty twenty-four        |

## Important Rules

- #### _Capitalize_ the names of _months_.
  - January, April, October
- #### Use _ordinal numbers_ when saying dates.
  - the **first** of May.
  - October **fifth.**
- #### Use **on** before a _specific date_.
  - My birthday is **on** July 10th.
  - The meeting is **on** March 21st.
- #### In _British English_, dates often include **the** and **of**.
  - **the** fifth **of** June.
  - **the** twenty-third **of** March.
- #### Remember that British and American English use _different date formats_.
  - 🇬🇧 15/10/2024 → 15 October 2024.
  - 🇺🇸 10/15/2024 → October 15, 2024.
