## What is Present Continuous?

The Present Continuous is used for actions happening right now or around the present time

📌 _Example_

- I am studying now
- She is eating

It describes actions **in progress**

---

## Form (Grammar Rules)

🏗️ _Structure_

- **Subject + verb “to be” + verb + -ing**

| Subject         | To be | Verb + ing |
| --------------- | ----- | ---------- |
| I               | am    | working    |
| He / She / It   | is    | working    |
| You / We / They | are   | working    |

Always use:

- **am / is / are**
- **verb + ing**

---

## Sentence Structure

**_Affirmative Sentences_**

🏗️ _Structure_

- **Subject + to be + verb-ing**

📌 _Example_

- I am studying
- She is working
- They are playing

**_Negative Sentences_**

🏗️ _Structure_

- **Subject + to be + not + verb-ing**

📌 _Example_

- I am not studying
- He is not working
- They are not playing

**_Interrogative Sentences_**

🏗️ _Structure_

- **To be + subject + verb-ing**

📌 _Example_

- Are you studying?
- Is she working?
- Are they playing?

---

## Uses of Present Continuous

1. Actions happening now

- I am studying now
- She is eating at the moment

---

2. Temporary actions (around now)

- They are learning English
- She is currently looking for a job

---

3. Planned future (near future)

- I am going to a party tonight
- We are working tomorrow

---

## Important Notes

**_Verbs NOT used in continuous_**

Some verbs are **not normally used with -ing**: know, like, love, want, need, believe

- Wrong: I am knowing him
- Correct: I know him

---

## Signal Words

Common expressions:

- now
- right now
- at the moment
- currently
- these days

---

## Quick Summary

- **am / is / are + verb-ing**
- Used for actions happening **now or temporarily**
- Questions → invert **to be**
- Some verbs don’t use continuous
