## What are Continuous Verb Tenses?

Continuous tenses describe actions that are:

- Happening now
- In progress
- Temporary

🏗️ _Structure_

- **to be + verb + -ing**

🏗️ _Examples_

- I am studying
- She is working

---

## Types of Verbs NOT used in Continuous

**_Verbs of Senses_**

- see
- hear
- smell
- taste
- feel

Incorrect: I am seeing you

Correct: I see you

**_Emotions & Mental States_**

- love
- like
- hate
- know
- believe
- understands
- want

Incorrect: I am knowing him

Correct: I know him

**_State / Abstract Verbs_**

- be
- need
- seem
- exist
- cost
- contain

Incorrect: He is needing help

Correct: He needs help

**_Possession Verbs_**

- have (possession)
- own
- belong
- possess

Incorrect: I am having a car

Correct: I have a car

---

## Special Cases (Important!)

**_Same verb, different meaning_**

Some verbs CAN be continuous depending on meaning:

- I **have** a car → possession (❌ continuous)
- I **am having** dinner → action (✔ continuous)

**_“To be” in continuous_**

Usually not used in continuous, BUT:

- You are silly → personality
- You are being silly → behavior (temporary)

---

## Key Concept

Difference:

- **Dynamic verbs** → actions → ✔ continuous
- **Stative verbs** → states → ❌ not continuous

---

## Quick Summary

- Continuous = action in progress
- Stative verbs usually NOT used with -ing
- Some verbs change meaning depending on use
