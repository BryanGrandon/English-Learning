## What is Present Simple?

The Present Simple is used to talk about:

- Daily routines
- Habits
- General facts

📌 _Examples_

- I study English
- She works in a hospital

---

## Form (Grammar Rules)

**_Base Rule_**

- **I / You / We / They → base verb**
- **He / She / It → verb + “-s”**

📌 _Examples_

- I work
- She works
- They play
- He plays

---

## Sentence Structure

**_Affirmative Sentences_**

🏗️ _Structure_

- **Subject + Verb**

📌 _Examples_

- I eat vegetables
- He plays football
- They learn English

**_Negative Sentences_**

🏗️ _Structure_

- **Subject + do/does + not + verb**

📌 _Examples_

- I do not (don’t) eat vegetables
- He does not (doesn’t) play football

**_Interrogative Sentences_**

🏗️ _Structure_

- **Do/Does + subject + verb**

📌 _Examples_

- Do you eat vegetables?
- Does he play football?

**_Important Rule_**

In negatives and questions:

- The **auxiliary (do/does)** changes
- The **main verb stays in base form**

---

## Uses of Present Simple

**_Routines and habits_**

- I wake up at 7:00
- She always studies at night

Common adverbs:

- always, usually, often, sometimes, never

**_General facts_**

- The sun rises in the east
- Elephants live in Africa

**_Scheduled future events_**

- The train leaves at 10:00
- The party is tonight

---

## Adverbs of Frequency

Usually go before the verb

- I always eat breakfast
- She often studies

Exception with **to be**:

- I am always happy
- He is often tired

---

## Quick Summary

- He/She/It → add **s**
- Negative → **do/does + not**
- Questions → **do/does at the beginning**
- Used for habits, facts, schedules
