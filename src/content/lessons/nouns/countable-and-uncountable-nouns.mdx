## What are Countable Nouns?

Countable nouns are nouns that can be counted.

They have singular and plural forms.

📌 _Examples._

- a pencil
- two cats
- three houses

📌 _Examples Sentences._

- I have **a pencil**.
- She has **three cats**.

---

## What are Uncountable Nouns?

Uncountable nouns are nouns that cannot be counted individually.

They usually represent substances, materials, liquids, or abstract ideas and do not have plural forms.

📌 _Examples._

---

- water
- sugar
- rice
- money
- information
- furniture

📌 _Examples Sentences._

- I drink **water** every day.
- She needs **information**.

---

## Grammar Rules

**Countable nouns have plural forms**

📌 _Examples._

- egg → eggs
- bicycle → bicycles
- dress → dresses

Use **a / an** with singular countable nouns

📌 _Examples._

- **an apple**
- **a house**
- ❌ a milk
- ✅ milk

**Use numbers with countable nouns**

📌 _Examples._

- **three apples**
- **five houses**
- ❌ two rices
- ✅ rice

---

## Making Uncountable Nouns Countable

Sometimes we can **make uncountable nouns countable** using **measure expressions**.

| Expression | Example          |
| ---------- | ---------------- |
| a piece of | a piece of bread |
| a cup of   | a cup of tea     |
| a glass of | a glass of wine  |
| a gram of  | a gram of salt   |

📌 _Examples sentences._

- I want **a cup of tea**.
- She bought **a piece of bread**.

---

## Common Mistakes

**_Using plural with uncountable nouns_**

- ❌ informations → ✅ information
- ❌ moneys → ✅ money

**_Using a / an with uncountable nouns_**

- ❌ a water → ✅ water
- ❌ a sugar → ✅ sugar

**_Using numbers with uncountable nouns_**

- ❌ two breads → ✅ two **pieces of bread**
- ❌ three rices → ✅ two **bowls of rice**
