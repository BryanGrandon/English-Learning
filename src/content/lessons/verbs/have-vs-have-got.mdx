## Meaning of _Have_ and _Have Got_

Both **have** and **have got** mean **“tener”** and are used to express **possession**.

📌 _Examples._

- I **have** two cats.
- I **have got** two cats.

They can also describe:

- **relationships**
- **illnesses**
- **physical characteristics**

📌 _Examples._

- She **has got** a cold.
- We **have** brown hair.

---

## Affirmative Form

**_Have_**

| Subject | Verb |
| ------- | ---- |
| I       | have |
| You     | have |
| He      | has  |
| She     | has  |
| It      | has  |
| We      | have |
| They    | have |

📌 _Examples._

- I **have** a car.
- She **has** a big house.
- They **have** two brothers.

**_Have Got_**

| Subject | Verb     | Short Form  |
| ------- | -------- | ----------- |
| I       | have got | I've got    |
| You     | have got | You've got  |
| He      | has got  | He's got    |
| She     | has got  | She's got   |
| It      | has got  | It's got    |
| We      | have got | We've got   |
| They    | have got | They've got |

📌 _Examples._

- I **have got** a car.
- She **has got** a cold.
- They **have got** two bikes.

---

## Negative Form

**_Have Got_**

🏗️ _Structure._

- subject + have/has + not + got

📌 _Examples._

- I **haven't got** a car.
- She **hasn't got** a cold.
- They **haven't got** pets.

**_Have_**

🏗️ _Structure._

- subject + do/does + not + have

📌 _Examples._

- I **don't have** a car.
- She **doesn't have** a bike.
- We **don't have** time.

---

## Interrogative Form

**_Have Got_**

🏗️ _Structure._

- have/has + subject + got

📌 _Examples._

- **Have you got** a car?
- **Has she got** a cold?
- **Have they got** any pets?

**_Have_**

🏗️ _Structure._

- do/does + subject + have

📌 _Examples._

- **Do you have** a car?
- **Does she have** a bike?
- **Do they have** children?

---

## Differences Between Have and Have Got

| Have                                         | Have Got                           |
| -------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| More common in **American English**          | More common in **British English** |
| Uses **do/does** for negatives and questions | Does **not need auxiliary do**     |
| Can be used in many tenses                   | Mainly used in **present simple**  |

📌 _Examples._

- **Do you have a car?**
- **Have you got a car?**

---

## Other Uses of Have

The verb **have** can also describe **actions or experiences**, not only possession.

📌 _Examples._

- **have breakfast / lunch / dinner**
- **have a shower / bath**
- **have a conversation**
- **have fun / have a good time**

📌 _Example sentences._

- I **have breakfast** at 7.
- They **have a party** tonight.
