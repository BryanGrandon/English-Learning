## What are Modal Verbs?

**Modal verbs** are **auxiliary verbs** that modify the meaning of another verb. They express ideas such as **ability, possibility, obligation, permission, or probability**.

Modal verbs **cannot function alone**; they must be followed by another verb in the **base form (infinitive without "to")**.

📌 _Examples._

- She **can play** the piano.
- I **might buy** this.

---

## Structure of Modal Verbs

Modal verbs follow this structure:

🏗️ _Structure._

- subject + modal verb + base verb

📌 _Examples._

- She **can swim**.
- They **must study**.
- We **should go**.

Important rules:

- The main verb **does not change**.
- Modal verbs **do not add -s in third person**.
- The verb after the modal **does not use "to"**.

📌 _Examples._

- ❌ She **cans swim**
- ✅ She **can swim**

---

## List of Modal Verbs

Common modal verbs include:

| Modal Verb | Meaning                       |
| ---------- | ----------------------------- |
| can        | ability / possibility         |
| could      | past ability / polite request |
| may        | permission / possibility      |
| might      | possibility                   |
| will       | future / intention            |
| shall      | suggestion / future (formal)  |
| should     | advice                        |
| ought to   | recommendation                |
| must       | obligation / necessity        |
| would      | polite request / conditional  |

These verbs help express different meanings depending on the context.

---

## Uses of Modal Verbs

**_Ability — Can_**

Used to express ability or possibility.

📌 _Examples._

- I **can speak** five languages.
- We **can work** late tonight.

Also used to ask permission:

- **Can I** have a glass of water?

**_Obligation — Must_**

Used to express **obligation or necessity**.

📌 _Examples._

- You **must read** this book.
- We **must leave** now

It can also express **probability**.

📌 _Examples._

- He **must be** sick.

**_Advice — Should / Ought to_**

Used to give recommendations or advice.

📌 _Examples._

- You **should study** more.
- She **ought to stop** smoking.

**_Polite Requests — Would_**

Used for polite requests or preferences.

📌 _Examples._

- **Would you help** me?
- I **would like** some coffee.

---

## Common Mistakes

**_Adding s to modal verbs_**

- ❌ She **cans** swim.
- ✅ She **can** swim.

**_Using to after a modal_**

- ❌ She can **to swim**.
- ✅ She can **swim**.

**_Using two modals together_**

- ❌ She **can must** go.
- ✅ She **must go**.
