Prepositions of place are used to express where something is.

They usually come after the main verb, especially the verb “to be”.

🏗️ _Structure_

- Subject + verb (to be) + preposition + place

📌 _Example._

- The book is **on** the table

## Common Prepositions

**_Position & proximity_**

| Preposition      | Meaning           | Example                                      |
| ---------------- | ----------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| next to / beside | al lado de        | The supermarket is next to the bank          |
| by               | cerca de          | I sit by the window                          |
| between          | entre (dos cosas) | The shop is between the bank and the station |

---

**_Direction & position_**

| Preposition | Meaning                | Example                              |
| ----------- | ---------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| behind      | detrás de              | The church is behind the school      |
| in front of | delante de             | The hotel is in front of the station |
| opposite    | enfrente (cara a cara) | The bank is opposite the market      |

---

**_Vertical position_**

| Preposition | Meaning            | Example                      |
| ----------- | ------------------ | ---------------------------- |
| under       | debajo de          | The ball is under the chair  |
| above       | encima (sin tocar) | The clock is above the table |
| below       | debajo (sin tocar) | The table is below the clock |

---

## Key Differences

**_Next to / Beside vs By_**

- _Next to / beside_ → directly at the side
- _By_ → near, not necessarily touching

---

**_In front of vs Opposite_**

- _In front of_ → delante (no necesariamente cara a cara)
- _Opposite_ → frente a frente

---

**_Above vs Below_**

- _Above_ → higher, no contact
- _Below_ → lower, no contact
