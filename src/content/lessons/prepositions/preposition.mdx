Prepositions are used to express **time, place, position, or direction**.

They are very common but can be difficult because they **depend on context and don’t translate directly**.

---

## Position Rule

Prepositions usually follow this structure:

🏗️ _Structure_

- Verb + Preposition + Noun

🏗️ _Examples._

- The cat is **in the box**
- The book is **on the table**
- She is **at the airport**

📌 After a preposition:

- ✔ noun → _in the room_
- ✔ -ing → _before eating_
- ❌ base verb

---

## Common Prepositions

**_IN ( inside / within )_**

Use for:

- months, years, periods
- inside spaces, cities, countries

Place:

- in May
- in a room
- in Chile

Time:

- in May
- in 2001
- in the morning

---

**_AT ( specific point )_**

Use for:

- exact places or points
- specific times

Place:

- at home
- at the airport

Time:

- at 8:00
- at Christmas

---

**_ON ( on a surface )_**

Use for:

- surfaces
- days and dates

Place:

- on the table
- on the wall

Time:

- on Monday
- on July 5th

---

## Types of Prepositions

**_Prepositions of Place_**

Show **where something is**

- The book is **on** the table
- The cat is **in** the box

📌 Usually used after the verb (often **to be**)

---

**_Prepositions of Time_**

Show **when something happens**

- in June
- on Monday
- at 8 o’clock

📌 Very common: **in / on / at**

---

**_Prepositions of Movement_**

Show **direction or movement**

- go **to**
- walk **through**
- jump **over**

---

## Common Mistakes

**_Translating prepositions literally from Spanish_**

- ❌ I live **in** the street
- ✅ I live **on** this street

---

**_Using a verb after a preposition_**

- ❌ before eat
- ✅ before **eating**
