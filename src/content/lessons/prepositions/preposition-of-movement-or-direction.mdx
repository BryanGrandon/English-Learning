## What are prepositions of movement

**Prepositions of movement or direction** are used to show **movement from one place to another**.

They usually appear with **verbs of movement**, such as:

- go
- walk
- run
- fly
- drive

📌 _Examples._

These prepositions normally appear **after the verb** and before the destination or place.

---

## Position in the sentence

Most sentences follow this structure:

📌 _Structure._

- Subject + verb of movement + preposition + place

📌 _Examples._

- She walked **through the park**.
- They ran **across the street**.
- We drove **into the city**.

## **_Common Mistakes_**

**_Confusing into and in_**

- **into** → movement inside
- **in** → position (no movement)

📌 _Examples._

- She walked **into** the room.
- She is **in** the room.

---

**_Confusing onto and on_**

- **onto** → movement to a surface
- **on** → position

📌 _Examples._

- The cat jumped **onto** the table.
- The cat is **on** the table.
