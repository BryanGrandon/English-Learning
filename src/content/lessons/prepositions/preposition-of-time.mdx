Prepositions of time are used to indicate when something happens.

📌 _Examples._

- Are you going to eat **before** or **after** the cinema?

---

## Position in the sentence

Prepositions of time usually follow this structure:

🏗️ _Structure._

---

- Verb + preposition + time expression

📌 _Examples._

- Call me **before** one.
- We will meet **after** the movie.

They normally appear **after verbs and before nouns or time expressions**

---

## Table of words for prepositions of time

| Work   | Meaning                           | Example                             |
| ------ | --------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| before | antes / antes de                  | Call me before one.                 |
| after  | después / después de              | We will meet after the movie.       |
| during | durante                           | Don’t talk during the move.         |
| for    | durante / por (periodo de tiempo) | I lived in England for three years. |

---

## **_Common Confusion_**

**_DURING vs FOR_**

Although both refer to time, they are used differently.

**During** → indicates **when something happens inside a period**

📌 _Examples._

- Don’t talk **during the movie**.

**For** → indicates **how long something lasts**

📌 _Examples._

- I studied **for two hours**.
