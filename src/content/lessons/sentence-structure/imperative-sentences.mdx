## What are Imperative Sentences?

Imperative sentences are used to:

- Give orders
- Give instructions
- Make requests
- Give warnings

📌 _Examples_

- Open the door
- Don't touch that

The subject is **not written** because it is always **“you”**

---

## Affirmative Imperatives

Used to give orders or instructions

🏗️ _Structure_

**Verb + complement**

📌 _Examples_

- Open the door
- Wash your hands
- Tell me the truth

---

## Negative Imperatives

Used to tell someone **NOT to do something**

🏗️ _Structure_

**Do not (don't) + verb + complement**

📌 _Examples_

- Do not lie
- Don't run
- Don't hit your sister

---

## Using “Let's” (Suggestions)

Used when we include ourselves (**we**)

🏗️ _Structure_

**Let's + verb**

📌 _Examples_

- Let's go
- Let's study
- Let's play football

---

### Negative with Let's

🏗️ _Structure_

---

**Let's not + verb**

📌 _Examples_

- Let's not fight
- Let's not argue

---

## Important Notes

- Imperatives can sound **strong or direct**
- Use **“please”** to be polite
  - Please sit down
  - Please help me
- They often start directly with a **verb**

---

## Quick Summary

- No subject (it’s always “you”)
- Verb in base form
- Negative → use **don’t**
- “Let’s” → suggestions
