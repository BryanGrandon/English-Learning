## Basic sentence structure

A sentence in English follows a clear structure:

🏗️ _Structure._

- **Subject + Verb + Complement**

- Subject → who does the action
- Verb → action
- Complement → extra information

🏗️ _Examples._

- She studies English
- They play soccer

---

## Affirmative Sentences

Used to express positive statements

🏗️ _Structure._

- **Subject + Verb + Complement**

🏗️ _Examples._

- I like pizza
- He works in an office
- We live in Chile

In affirmative sentences, the subject usually comes before the verb.

---

## Negative Sentences

Used to express something is not true, English usually uses the auxiliary **“do”**.

🏗️ _Structure._

- **Subject + do/does + not + base verb**

🏗️ _Examples._

- She does not like ice cream.
- We do not live in Madrid.
- They do not have a car.

Short forms:

| Full     | Contraction |
| -------- | ----------- |
| do not   | don't       |
| does not | doesn't     |

The **main verb stays in the base form**.

---

## Special Case: To Be / Have Got

With **to be** and **have got**, we **do not use “~~do/does”**.~~

**_To Be_**

🏗️ _Structure._

- Subject + am/is/are + not

🏗️ _Examples._

- I am not happy.
- They have not got a car.

**_Have Got_**

🏗️ _Structure._

- Subject + have/has + not got

🏗️ _Examples._

- I have not got a car
- He has not got a dog

---

## Interrogative Sentences (Questions)

Questions usually begin with an **auxiliary verb**.

🏗️ _Structure._

- Do/Does + subject + verb

🏗️ _Examples._

- Do you like pizza?
- Does she work here?

In questions, the **verb comes before the subject**.

---

## Questions with To Be / Have Got

**_To Be_**

🏗️ _Structure._

- Am/Is/Are + subject

🏗️ _Examples._

- Are you tired?
- Is she happy?

**_Have Got_**

🏗️ _Structure._

- Have/Has + subject + got

🏗️ _Examples._

- Have you got a car?
- Has he got a dog?
