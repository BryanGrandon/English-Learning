# The Indefinite Articles

Use a and an to talk about a person, thing, or idea that is not specific, not known, or mentioned for the first time.

## Indefinite Articles: “a” & “an”

**A** and **an** are the indefinite articles in English.

They are equivalent to **un** and **una** in Spanish and are used only with singular countable nouns.

- I saw a dog in the park.
- She is a teacher.
- He bought an apple.

Use indefinite articles only with:

- singular nouns
- countable nouns

We often use a or an when introducing something for the first time.

I saw a dog.
The dog was very friendly.

## When to use **a** or **an**

The choice between a and an depends on the sound that follows, not the spelling.

    - a car

Remember: a and an depend on pronunciation, not on the first letter of the word.

- ✔ a university
- ✔ an hour

## Position Rule

The indefinite article a or an comes before a singular noun.

If there is an adjective, the article comes before the adjective.

<LessonBlock variant='structure'> a / an + (adjective) + singular noun</LessonBlock>

<LessonBlock variant="example">

- a house
- a red car
- an apple
- an interesting book

</LessonBlock>

## Important Exceptions

Remember: a and an depend on pronunciation, not spelling.

### Words that begin with the /juː/ ("yu") sound

Although these words start with u or eu, they begin with a consonant sound, so we use a.

- a university
- a uniform
- a European

### Words with a silent h

When the h is not pronounced, the word begins with a vowel sound, so we use an.

- an hour
- an honest person
- an honor

### Use a when the h is pronounced.

- a hospital
- a hotel
- a house

## Uses of a and an

### To talk about something in general

Use a or an when referring to a person or thing that is not specific.

- He has a computer.
- We work in a school.
- I want an orange.

### To mean "one"

A and an can mean one.

- I want an apple.
- There are a hundred students.
- She has a brother.

### When mentioning something for the first time

Use a or an when introducing a noun for the first time. After that, use the to refer to the same noun.

- I live in an apartment.
- The apartment is big.
- First mention → a / an
- Later mentions → the

### For professions and jobs

Use a or an before a profession or job title.

- Maria is a doctor.
- Juan is an architect.
- My father is a teacher.

## Common Mistake

**_Using a/an with plural nouns_**

- ❌ a cars
- ✅ cars

---

**_Using a before vowels_**

- ❌ a apple
- ✅ an apple
