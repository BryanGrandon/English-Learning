## What are Adjectives?

An **adjective** is a word that **describes or modifies a noun**. It gives more information about a person, place, thing, or idea.

📌 _Examples._

- a **big** house
- two **tall** men
- **long** hair

📌 _Example Sentences._

- The **tall** man is my teacher.
- She has a **happy** child.
- The **red** ball is on the table.

---

## Types of Adjectives

In English there are **six main types of adjectives**.

| Type                          | Description                                     | Examples                 | Example Sentence                |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | ------------------------ | ------------------------------- |
| **Descriptive / Qualitative** | Describe qualities or characteristics of a noun | big, blue, young, hot    | She has a **beautiful** house.  |
| **Demonstrative**             | Point to specific things                        | this, that, these, those | **This** book is interesting.   |
| **Quantitative**              | Express quantity or amount                      | some, any, many, much    | There are **many** people here. |
| **Interrogative**             | Used to ask questions                           | which, what, where, how  | **Which** car is yours?         |
| **Possessive**                | Show ownership or possession                    | my, your, his, our       | This is **my** house.           |
| **Numeric**                   | Express numbers or order                        | one, four, first, third  | She has **three** dogs.         |

---

## Position of Adjectives

Adjectives usually appear **before the noun** they describe.

🏗️ _Structure._

- adjective + noun

📌 _Examples._

- a **happy child**
- a **dark street**
- a **cold winter**

Sometimes adjectives appear **after certain verbs**, such as:

- **to be**
- **to feel**
- **to look**
- **to seem**
- **to smell**
- **to taste**

📌 _Examples._

- She **looks tired**.
- I **feel good**.
- You **are beautiful**.

---

## Adjective Rules

Adjectives do not change for gender

📌 _Examples._

- a **tall** man
- a **tall** woman

The adjective stays the same.

Adjectives usually do not have plural forms

📌 _Examples._

- a **red** car
- two **red** cars

The adjective **does not change**.

Multiple adjectives can be used together

📌 _Examples._

- a **small black book**
- a **pretty Spanish woman**
- a **large glass table**

---

## Order of Adjectives

When using multiple adjectives, English usually follows this order.

| Order | Type        | Examples             |
| ----- | ----------- | -------------------- |
| 1     | opinion     | beautiful, wonderful |
| 2     | size        | big, small, tall     |
| 3     | age         | old, young, new      |
| 4     | shape       | round, square        |
| 5     | color       | red, blue, green     |
| 6     | origin      | Spanish, American    |
| 7     | material    | cotton, silver       |
| 8     | purpose/use | electric, political  |

📌 _Examples._

- a **beautiful big old house**

---

## Common Mistakes

**Putting adjectives after nouns**

- ❌ a car red
- ✅ a **red car**

**Adding plural to adjectives**

- ❌ reds cars
- ✅ **red cars**

**Incorrect adjective order**

- ❌ a wooden old chair
- ✅ an **old wooden chair**
