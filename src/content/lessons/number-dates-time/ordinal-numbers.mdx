## What are Ordinal Numbers?

Ordinal numbers are used to show the **position or order** of something in a sequence.

👉 Examples:

- first, second, third
- fourth, fifth

📌 _Examples Sentence_

- She finished **first** in the race.
- Today is my **third** day here.

## How to Form Ordinal Numbers

**_General Rule_**

Add **-th** to the number:

- four → fourth
- six → sixth
- ten → tenth

**_Exceptions (memorize)_**

- one → **first**
- two → **second**
- three → **third**

Other spelling changes:

- five → **fifth**
- nine → **ninth**
- twelve → **twelfth**

## Compound Ordinals (21–99)

📏 _Rule_

- Only the last number changes.

- 21 → twenty-**first**
- 32 → thirty-**second**
- 45 → forty-**fifth**

---

## Abbreviations

Use number + last letters:

- 1st → first
- 2nd → second
- 3rd → third
- 4th → fourth
- 21st → twenty-first

---

## Uses of Ordinal Numbers

**_Dates_**

- April **5th**
- the **first** of May

**_Position / Ranking_**

- He is **second** in the competition

**_Floors / Order_**

- She lives on the **third** floor

---

## Tips

- Use **“the”** before ordinal numbers
  - _the first, the second_
- Only change the **last part** in big numbers
- Memorize the **irregular forms (1–3)**
