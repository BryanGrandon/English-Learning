## What is Time?

Time is used to express **hours and minutes** during the day.

📌 _Example_

- 3:00 → three o’clock
- 6:30 → six thirty

---

## Asking the Time

Common questions:

- What time is it?
- What’s the time?
- Do you have the time?

---

## Exact Hours (o’clock)

Use **o’clock** for exact hours:

- 1:00 → one o’clock
- 5:00 → five o’clock

Only use _o’clock_ when there are **no minutes**

---

## Minutes (Digital Form)

Say: **hour + minutes**

- 3:10 → three ten
- 6:25 → six twenty-five
- 8:05 → eight “oh” five

---

## Past and To

There are 2 main patterns:

**_Minutes past (1–30)_**

Use **past**

- 3:10 → ten past three
- 2:20 → twenty past two

**_Minutes to (31–59)_**

Use **to**

- 3:50 → ten to four
- 2:45 → fifteen to three

After 30 minutes, we talk about the **next hour**

---

## Special Expressions

- 15 minutes → **(a) quarter past**
  - 4:15 → quarter past four
- 30 minutes → **half past**
  - 6:30 → half past six
- 45 minutes → **(a) quarter to**
  - 7:45 → quarter to eight

---

## a.m. and p.m.

Use to show time of day:

- a.m. → morning
- p.m. → afternoon / night

📌 _Example_

- 6:00 a.m. → six in the morning
- 8:00 p.m. → eight at night

---

## Parts of the Day

- morning → 6:00 – 12:00
- afternoon → 12:00 – 18:00
- evening → 18:00 – 21:00
- night → 21:00 – 6:00

---

## Tips

- Say **hour first**, then minutes
- Use **o’clock only for exact hours**
- Learn both:
  - digital (3:45 → three forty-five)
  - traditional (quarter to four)
- Practice with real clocks ⏰
