## What are Cardinal Numbers?

Cardinal numbers are used to **count things and express quantity**.

Examples:

- one, two, three
- ten, twenty, one hundred

They are essential for:

- prices
- phone numbers
- dates
- age

📌 _Example_

- I have **three** books.
- She is **twenty** years old.

## Tens (20–90)

📏 _Rule_

- They end in **-ty**

- twenty → 20
- thirty → 30
- forty → 40
- fifty → 50
- sixty → 60
- seventy → 70
- eighty → 80
- ninety → 90

---

## Compound Numbers (21–99)

📏 _Rule_

- tens + "-" + number

- twenty-one → 21
- thirty-two → 32
- forty-five → 45

📌 _Example_

- I am **twenty-one** years old.

---

## Hundreds, Thousands, Millions

**_Hundreds_**

- one hundred
- two hundred

**_With "and"_**

- one hundred **and** twenty
- three hundred **and** fifty-five

**_Thousands_**

- one thousand
- ten thousand

**_Millions_**

- one million

---

## Tips

- Use **"and"** between hundreds and tens
- Use **hyphen (-)** for numbers like twenty-one
- Memorize 1–12 first
