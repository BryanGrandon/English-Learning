## What is the Date?

The date is used to express **day, month, and year**.

📌 _Example_

- 5 October 2024
- July 4, 2024

---

## How to Say the Date

In English, we use **ordinal numbers** when speaking dates

📌 _Example_

- It’s the **first of May**
- It’s **June the fifth**
- It’s the **twenty-third of March**

---

## Two Common Formats

**_British English_**

**Day + Month + Year**

- 15 October 2024
- Spoken: _the fifteenth of October_

**_American English_**

Month + Day + Year

- October 15, 2024
- Spoken: _October fifteenth_

---

## Writing the Date

1. With words

- 1st June
- June 1st

2. With numbers

- 01/06/2024 (UK)
- 06/01/2024 (US)

---

## Important Rules

- Use **capital letters** for months
- Use **ordinal numbers** when speaking
- Use **“the”** and **“of”** in British English
- Be careful with format (UK vs US)

---

## Asking the Date

Common questions:

- What’s the date today?
- What date is it?

Answers:

- It’s the **first of June**
- It’s **June the first**

---

## Talking About Years

📌 _Example_

- 1999 → nineteen ninety-nine
- 2005 → two thousand and five
- 2024 → twenty twenty-four

---

## Tips

- Always **say ordinal numbers** (first, second, third…)
- Don’t confuse **UK and US formats**
- Practice both ways of saying dates
