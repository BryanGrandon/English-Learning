# Personal Pronouns

Personal pronouns are short words used to replace nouns that refer to people, places, or things, in order to avoid
repetition. They vary according to person, number, gender, and grammatical case (subject or object).

- [Obj](#object-pronouns)

## Subject Pronouns

Subject pronouns are personal pronouns that **perform the action** in a sentence.

<section className='grid grid-cols-1 gap-4 sm:grid-cols-2'>
  <LessonBlock variant='position'>
    - Before the verb
  </LessonBlock>

  <LessonBlock variant='structure'>    
  - *Subject* + verb + Complement      
  </LessonBlock>
</section>

| Word | Meaning           | Example                     |
| ---- | ----------------- | --------------------------- |
| I    | yo                | **I** am ill.               |
| you  | tú, usted         | **You** are tall.           |
| he   | él                | **He** is handsome.         |
| she  | ella              | **She** is pretty.          |
| it   | ello (neutral)    | **It** is cold today.       |
| we   | nosotros          | **We** are tired.           |
| you  | vosotros, ustedes | **You** are angry.          |
| they | ellos, ellas      | **They** are at the cinema. |

## Object Pronouns

Object pronouns replace the object in a sentence, that is, the person or thing that receives the action of the verb.

<section className='grid grid-cols-1 gap-4 sm:grid-cols-2'>
  <LessonBlock variant='position'>
      - After the verb they complement.
      - After prepositions such as: **for**, **to**, **with**, **in**.
  </LessonBlock>

  <LessonBlock variant='structure'>    
      - *Subject* + verb + *Object Pronoun*
      - Preposition + *Object Pronoun*
  </LessonBlock>
</section>

| Word | Meaning               | Example               |
| ---- | --------------------- | --------------------- |
| me   | mi                    | Can you help **me**?  |
| you  | a ti, a usted         | I can help **you**.   |
| him  | a él                  | Can you see **him**?  |
| her  | a ella                | Give it to **her**.   |
| it   | a ello                | Give **it** a kick.   |
| us   | a nosotros            | Can you see **us**?   |
| you  | a vosotros, a ustedes | I see **you**.        |
| them | a ellos               | He can help **them**. |

## Neutral Form

In English, pronouns distinguish gender:

- **He** → male
- **She** → female
- **It** → neutral (things, animals, or situations)

The _neutral form_ is used when referring to things, ideas, situations, weather, time, distance, or when gender is unknown or irrelevant.

<section className='grid grid-cols-1 gap-4 lg:grid-cols-2'>

| Use of it | Examples                  |
| --------- | ------------------------- |
| Weather.  | It is raining.            |
| Time.     | What time is it?          |
| Distance. | It is far.                |
| Things.   | Where is it _[the book]_? |

  <LessonBlock variant='important'>

      - English usually requires a subject pronoun.
      <div className='ml-4'>
        - ❌ **_Is raining._**
        - ✅ **_It is raining._**
      </div>

      - Use **it** for singular things and **they** for plural things.
      <div className='ml-4'>
        - The book is new. **It** is interesting.
        - The books are new. **They** are interesting.
      </div>

  </LessonBlock>
</section>

### Subject vs Object Pronouns

- The **subject** does the action.
- The **object** receives the action.

| 🟦 Subject | Example          | 🟣 Object | Example            |
| ---------- | ---------------- | --------- | ------------------ |
| I          | _I am tired._    | me        | _She called me._   |
| you        | _You are late._  | you       | _I saw you._       |
| he         | _He is happy._   | him       | _I know him._      |
| she        | _She is here._   | her       | _I helped her._    |
| it         | _It is new._     | it        | _I found it._      |
| we         | _We are ready._  | us        | _They invited us._ |
| they       | _They are busy._ | them      | _I saw them._      |
