### Subject Pronouns

Subject pronouns are personal pronouns that **perform the action** in a sentence.

**Position**

- Before the verb

**Structure**

- _Subject_ + verb + Complement

| Word | Meaning           | Example                 |
| ---- | ----------------- | ----------------------- |
| I    | yo                | I am ill.               |
| you  | tú, usted         | You are tall.           |
| he   | él                | He is handsome.         |
| she  | ella              | She is pretty.          |
| it   | ello (neutral)    | It is cold today.       |
| we   | nosotros          | We are tired.           |
| you  | vosotros, ustedes | You are angry.          |
| they | ellos, ellas      | They are at the cinema. |

### Object Pronouns

Object pronouns replace the object in a sentence, that is, the person or thing that receives the action of the verb.

**Position**

- After the verb they complement.
  After prepositions such as: for, to, with, in.

**Structure**

- _Subject_ + verb + Object Pronoun
- Preposition + Object Pronoun

| Word | Meaning               | Example           |
| ---- | --------------------- | ----------------- |
| me   | mi                    | Can you help me?  |
| you  | a ti, a usted         | I can help you.   |
| him  | a él                  | Can you see him?  |
| her  | a ella                | Give it to her.   |
| it   | a ello                | Give it a kick.   |
| us   | a nosotros            | Can you see us?   |
| you  | a vosotros, a ustedes | I see you.        |
| them | a ellos               | He can help them. |

### Neutral Form

In English, pronouns distinguish gender:

- He → male
- She → female
- It → neutral (things / unknown gender)

The **neutral form** is used when referring to things, concepts, situations, or when gender is unknown or irrelevant.

| Use of it | Examples                |
| --------- | ----------------------- |
| Weather.  | It is raining.          |
| Time.     | What time is it?        |
| Distance. | It is far.              |
| Things.   | Where is it [the book]? |

**Important**

- In English, **“it” is required as a subject**, even when Spanish omits it.
  - ❌ _Is raining._
  - ✅ _It is raining._
- When referring to things in plural, English uses **they/them**.
  - It is new. (the book)
  - They are new. (the books)

### Subject vs Object Pronouns

Many mistakes happen because learners confuse subject and object pronouns.

#### Common Mistakes

❌ Me am tired.
✅ I am tired.

❌ She gave I a gift.
✅ She gave me a gift.
